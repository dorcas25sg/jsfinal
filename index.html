An Aging Nation...

Toggle the population pyramid*. What do you notice?

  • The bulk of the population moves upwards
  • A widening top as people live longer
  • A narrowing bottom as people have fewer children

The proportion of elderly in Singapore (aged 65 and above)
has doubled from 6% in 1990 to 13% in 2015 and is expected
to continue rising to 24% in 2030**.

Planning and infrastructure needs to keep up with the changes!

* Population data taken from the Department of Statistics, Singapore

** Based on a report by the Ministry of Health Aging Planning Office in 2015

As A Response...

Singapore has initiated a range of programs and infrastructures as part of the ‘aging in place’ approach to promote growing old within your home, neighborhood, and community, rather than overloading existing nursing facilities and tightening land availability.
